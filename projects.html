Emojis and Weather

For our machine learning final, we were placed into groups, and asked to apply machine learning to a novel dataset. Emojis provide an easy way of exracting the sentiment of a tweet without the hassle of analyzing natural language. We decided to predict weather conditions given the distribution of emojis used in a given area.

Learning the McGurk Effect from Raw Input

Applying a multimodal convolutional autoencoder, with top-down feedback and lateral inhibition, to pairs of phonemes and visemes. The goal of the project is to learn a joint embedding space such that when the trained model is fed a mismatched phoneme/viseme pair, it exhibits the McGurk Effect.
